About Me

Associate Professor at Rotterdam School of Management, Erasmus University Rotterdam

Primary research focus: Economic consequences of reporting transparency
Currently, I am interested in how disclosure practices affect individual and/or market behavior and corporate outcomes.

My research has been published in leading journals including Journal of Financial Economics, The Accounting Review, and The Review of Financial Studies, investigating how information environments impact corporate misconduct, industry coordination, and mergers & acquisitions, with a particular focus on the interplay between information asymmetry and regulatory frameworks.

Research Highlights

Local Newspaper Closures & Corporate Misconduct

Our research reveals that when local newspapers cease operations, corporate environmental violations increase by 1.1% and penalties drop by 15.2%, indicating reduced monitoring leads to increased corporate misconduct.

Reporting Transparency & Industry Coordination

Data analysis confirms enhanced reporting transparency reduces cartel duration by 38%, demonstrating disclosure requirements can disrupt collusive arrangements and promote competition.
